<p><p>MEA (Membrane Electrode Assembly) and PEM (Proton Exchange Membrane) fuel cells are essential components in hydrogen fuel cell technology, primarily used for converting chemical energy into electrical energy. MEA consists of an ion-conductive membrane sandwiched between two electrodes, facilitating the electrochemical reaction that powers the fuel cell. PEM fuel cells utilize a solid polymer electrolyte to conduct protons while remaining impermeable to gases, making them highly efficient and suitable for various applications, including transportation and stationary power generation.</p><p>The MEA & PEM fuel cell market is gaining traction due to increasing demands for clean and renewable energy solutions, advancements in fuel cell technologies, and supportive government policies aimed at reducing carbon emissions.
