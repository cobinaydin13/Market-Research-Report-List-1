<p><p>The digital dental cameras market comprises primarily two types: intraoral and extraoral cameras. Intraoral cameras are handheld devices used by dentists to capture detailed images of a patient's oral cavity, aiding in diagnostics and treatment planning. Extraoral cameras, on the other hand, are typically larger and used to photograph the face and jaw, providing broader visual context for orthodontic and prosthetic evaluations. Both types enhance patient communication, improve documentation, and facilitate better clinical outcomes in dental practice.</p></p>
