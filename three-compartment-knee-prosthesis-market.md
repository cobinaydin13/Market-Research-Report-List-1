<p><p>The three-compartment knee prosthesis market is primarily segmented into cemented and non-cemented bone types. Cemented prostheses utilize a bone cement for secure fixation, offering stability and immediate load-bearing capacity, ideal for patients with good bone quality. In contrast, non-cemented prostheses rely on the biological bonding of the implant with the bone, promoting long-term integration and favored in younger, more active patients. Each type caters to different patient needs, surgical preferences, and activity levels, influencing choice and performance outcomes.</p></p>
