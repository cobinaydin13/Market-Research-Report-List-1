<p><p>The albumin market consists of three main types: Human Serum Albumin (HSA), Bovine Serum Albumin (BSA), and Recombinant Albumin. HSA is derived from human blood and is primarily used in medical applications like transfusions and drug delivery. BSA, sourced from cattle, serves as a laboratory reagent and in vaccines. Recombinant Albumin is genetically engineered and offers a safer alternative, reducing the risk of transmitting diseases. Each type caters to different sectors, including healthcare, pharmaceuticals, and research.</p></p>
